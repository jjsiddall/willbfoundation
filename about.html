About Will

In 1999, when Will and his twin sister, Alexis, were born, there was no newborn screening for Cystic Fibrosis (CF), so Will went many years without being treated for his disease. Will was (finally) given a test and just before he turned 10, Will was diagnosed with Cystic Fibrosis. Not long after Will found out he had CF; Will asked his parents if he could start a charity to help cure the disease. Inspired by how Will reacted to having CF, along with his heartfelt request, The Will B Foundation was created.

CF

Cystic Fibrosis is a genetic disease that affects the lungs and digestive system. This disease is a rare disease affecting about 30,000 children and adults in the United States. Will’s cells produce thick and sticky mucus that can make him susceptible to lung infections and complicate the way he digests his food. For a couple of hours each day, Will inhales several medicines through a nebulizer and completes breathing exercises that help keep his lungs clear. Learn More »

Today

Today, Will is a 14 year old teenage boy who pushes himself every day to stay healthy. He recently graduated from St. Walter Grade School in Roselle, Illinois where he maintained high honor roll every quarter. He continues to play his three favorite sports of tennis, basketball and golf. And what Will wants everyone to know is he is a passionate young man on a mission to help find a cure for CF.

There "Will B" a Cure

Currently, there is no cure for CF. Will believes there WILL B a cure and he has entrusted supporters of The Will B Foundationto be part of the solution. We won’t let him down. Together, and dollar by dollar, we can and will make a difference so that one day soon there Will B a cure for Cystic Fibrosis.
